## Page Responsibilities

### Home

**Must:**

- introduce Francis and his voice quickly;
- establish the Copywriter & Copyeditor positioning;
- preview selected work;
- communicate the Write it / Shape it / Sharpen it capability;
- provide concise professional credibility;
- lead naturally to contact.

**Should not:**

- become a full CV;
- list every service;
- preview every piece of work;
- over-explain the positioning.

### Work

**Must:**

- present the six selected entries;
- make range easy to understand;
- remain curated and editorial.

**Should not:**

- behave like a database;
- include filters or search;
- include excluded work just to make the portfolio feel larger.

### Individual Work Pages

**Must:**

- centre the writing;
- provide only genuine context;
- use editorial presentation rather than a wall of text;
- vary treatment according to the kind of work.

**Should not:**

- invent briefs;
- invent process;
- invent strategy;
- invent metrics;
- force every project into the same case-study template.

### About

**Must:**

- connect Francis’s writing, editing, quality-control, training, and content-compliance experience;
- show a coherent professional evolution;
- communicate personality and writing philosophy;
- include relevant experience and education without becoming a reformatted CV.

**Core story direction:**

> He started by writing the words. Then he spent years examining how words work, where they fail, and how to make other writers better.

This is a narrative direction, not locked final copy.

### Contact

**Must:**

- feel warm and human;
- state the kinds of opportunities Francis is open to;
- provide email and LinkedIn;
- make starting a conversation easy.

**Possible:**

- short contact form.

**Do not add:**

- booking system;
- elaborate project questionnaire;
- unnecessary lead-capture mechanics.
