# Portfolio Narrative

## Purpose

This portfolio exists to present Francis Igwe as an experienced Copywriter & Copyeditor through a focused body of work and a distinctive professional voice.

It is a portfolio for one real person. It is not a SaaS product, website builder, template system, marketplace, or documentation framework.

The portfolio should help a potential employer, client, or collaborator quickly understand:

- who Francis is;
- what kind of writing and editing work he can do;
- how he thinks about words;
- the range and quality of his work;
- why his copyediting and quality-control experience strengthens his value as a writer;
- how to contact him.

The work itself should remain the primary proof.

---

## Professional Positioning

**Name:** Francis Igwe

**Professional title:** Copywriter & Copyeditor

Francis should not be positioned as a writer for one industry. His available work spans hospitality, consumer education, business thought leadership, sales, advertising, scripts, and short-form promotional copy.

He should also not be positioned with the generic claim that he is simply a “versatile writer who writes for every industry.”

The stronger positioning is:

> Francis writes, shapes, and sharpens ideas so they are clearer, more engaging, and better suited to the job they need to do.

His advantage is that he can work on both sides of the writing process:

- create original copy;
- shape complicated or dull ideas into engaging content;
- edit existing writing for clarity, structure, flow, tone, and quality.

A useful internal expression of this is:

> He can create the words. He can also make existing words better.

This is positioning guidance, not necessarily final website copy.

## Central Portfolio Narrative

The portfolio should not tell the story of:

> A writer who can write many different things.

It should tell the story of:

> A writer who knows that how something is said can change how it is understood, felt, and remembered.

Francis has written to make people hungry, explain why a material matters, make a business idea interesting, sell a service, and make an investment proposition easier to understand.

His professional experience adds another layer: he has also spent years reviewing other writers’ work, improving structure and flow, maintaining quality, training writers, and working with content standards.

The formats change. The underlying job remains:

> Find the clearest, sharpest, most engaging way to say the thing.

## Voice Direction

The portfolio voice is:

**Witty. Sophisticated. Warm.**

These qualities must be applied with restraint.

### Witty

Humour should come from observation, phrasing, contrast, and the occasional unexpected line.

Use wit in:

- strong headlines;
- small transitions;
- project introductions;
- occasional button copy;
- selective microcopy.

Do not make every sentence a joke.

### Sophisticated

Sophistication should come from restraint, clarity, pacing, and confidence.

Do not copy the punctuation habits of some older samples into the portfolio voice. Repeated ellipses, excessive exclamation marks, and constant emphasis should not define the website.

The portfolio should feel confident enough not to shout.

### Warm

Francis should sound like a person speaking to another person.

Prefer:

- contractions;
- direct language;
- natural rhythm;
- occasional first person;
- clear sentences.

Avoid:

- corporate jargon;
- inflated claims;
- stiff professional language;
- unnecessary abstraction.

## Role of the Work

The portfolio is not an archive.

Only selected work should appear.

Each project should answer a simple question:

> What does this piece prove about Francis as a writer or editor?

The work should be curated to demonstrate different capabilities, not included simply because it exists.

---

## Project Page Principle

Dedicated project pages must not become walls of text.

The writing remains the centre of each page, but the presentation should use an editorial approach appropriate to the specific piece.

Possible treatments include:

- strong excerpts;
- oversized lines;
- pull quotes;
- typographic pacing;
- short context blocks;
- visual breaks;
- relevant imagery where genuinely useful;
- selective full-copy reveals;
- varied composition based on the type of work.

Do not force one repeated case-study template onto every project.

For substantial projects, the general content logic is:

1. Project introduction
2. Context
3. The writing challenge, only where genuinely known
4. The writing itself
5. Brief reflection, only where useful

Do not invent:

- client briefs;
- strategy;
- process;
- challenges;
- results;
- metrics.

If context is limited, say less.

---

## Editing Existing Samples

The portfolio may lightly edit samples for:

- obvious grammar;
- punctuation;
- readability;
- presentation consistency.

Do not rewrite a sample so heavily that it stops representing Francis’s actual work.

Where a sample contains factual, scientific, financial, health, or performance claims, the portfolio should not present those claims as current verified facts unless they have been checked.

The purpose is to showcase the writing.
