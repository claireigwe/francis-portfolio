# Content Rules

## Purpose

These rules govern how content is used, edited, implemented, and published in Francis Igwe’s portfolio.

For the actual copy, use the files in:

`content/`

For positioning and voice, use:

`.agents/context/portfolio-narrative.md`

For available work and known limitations, use:

`.agents/context/content-inventory.md`

Do not duplicate those sources here.

---

## Source of Truth

The files in `content/` are the source of truth for public website copy.

Do not replace them with:

- invented copy;
- placeholder copy;
- AI-generated alternatives;
- text written directly inside page components without a clear reason.

When implementation and content files disagree, preserve the content files unless explicitly instructed otherwise.

---

## Before Using Content

Before implementing a page:

1. Read the relevant content file completely.
2. Identify public content and internal notes.
3. Preserve the intended order and narrative.
4. Check for verification warnings.
5. Use the visual direction to determine composition.

Do not implement from isolated excerpts when the full file provides necessary context.

---

## Content Integrity

Do not invent:

- clients;
- briefs;
- audiences;
- campaign contexts;
- responsibilities;
- dates;
- results;
- metrics;
- testimonials;
- awards;
- project outcomes;
- publication history.

Do not turn assumptions into facts.

Do not imply knowledge of an original brief when the brief is unavailable.

Do not exaggerate Francis’s role or experience.

---

## Original Work

Francis’s original writing is portfolio evidence.

Do not substantially rewrite it during implementation.

Do not:

- modernise his voice;
- make every piece sound alike;
- remove unusual phrasing merely because it is unconventional;
- replace his writing with cleaner AI-generated copy.

Preserve the differences between:

- long-form articles;
- sales copy;
- scripts;
- headlines;
- short copy.

The portfolio should demonstrate range, not artificial consistency.

---

## Editorial Corrections

Use editorial corrections already approved in the content files.

Do not silently perform large rewrites.

Minor corrections may be made only when they fix an obvious:

- typo;
- punctuation error;
- spacing error;
- formatting inconsistency.

Do not change meaning, tone, claims, or structure without explicit approval.

When uncertain, preserve the source.

---

## Portfolio Commentary

Portfolio commentary may explain:

- what the piece demonstrates;
- how the writing behaves;
- what makes the piece interesting;
- what a visitor should notice.

Do not present commentary as factual knowledge of:

- the original brief;
- the client’s internal goals;
- the target audience;
- campaign performance;
- business results;

unless the source material confirms it.

Use careful language when context is incomplete.

---

## Markdown Content

The Markdown files are structured editorial source material.

They are not generic blog posts.

Do not assume every:

- heading;
- paragraph;
- list;
- quotation;

must map to one universal visual component.

Preserve the content and its intended order while allowing the implementation to create a deliberate composition.

Do not build a generic publishing system that flattens all pages into one format.

---

## Public Content and Internal Notes

Some content files may contain internal instructions, warnings, or verification notes.

These must remain available to the build process but must not appear as public portfolio copy.

Do not publicly render:

- verification warnings;
- implementation instructions;
- editorial notes;
- asset reminders;
- unresolved questions;
- source-management notes.

Do not delete them from the source merely because they are not public.

---

## Claims and Verification

Treat unsupported claims carefully.

Flagged material may include:

- scientific claims;
- environmental claims;
- health claims;
- historical claims;
- business figures;
- financial returns;
- product certifications;
- sales claims.

Do not remove verification warnings.

Do not present an unverified claim as a fact endorsed by the portfolio.

Before publication, unresolved flagged claims must be:

- verified;
- carefully contextualised;
- edited with approval;
- or withheld from public display.

Do not invent citations or sources.

---

## Risky Source Material

Some original work may contain claims or language that should not be amplified without context.

The portfolio may show the writing as a work sample, but implementation must not accidentally transform the portfolio into an endorsement of the underlying claim.

Preserve the distinction between:

- displaying Francis’s work;
- asserting that every statement in the work is independently verified.

When uncertain, report the issue before publishing.

---

## Excerpts

Use excerpts already defined in the content files where available.

When a new excerpt is genuinely required:

- select directly from the source;
- preserve the original wording;
- keep enough context to avoid changing the meaning.

Do not rewrite an excerpt into a more impressive claim.

Do not use misleading fragments.

---

## Headlines and Short Copy

Preserve the rhythm of short-form writing.

Do not automatically:

- remove repetition;
- normalise punctuation;
- shorten unusual lines;
- combine separate options;
- convert every line to sentence case.

Headline alternatives and short-copy specimens may need to remain distinct because comparison is part of the portfolio value.

---

## Ellipses and Expressive Punctuation

Some original work uses repeated periods or extended pauses.

Do not automatically normalise every instance during implementation.

Treat them as part of the original writing unless the content file contains an approved correction.

CSS and layout may help present unusual punctuation cleanly.

Do not silently rewrite the voice to fit the design.

---

## Truncation

Do not truncate meaningful content merely because it is difficult to design.

Do not hide substantial copy behind:

- tiny scroll areas;
- inaccessible accordions;
- hover states;
- arbitrary character limits.

A preview may use an excerpt.

A dedicated project page should preserve the intended work.

If the full piece creates a design problem, solve the composition rather than deleting the writing.

---

## Content Order

Preserve the intended narrative order unless the content file or visual direction explicitly allows another sequence.

Do not rearrange sections merely to fit a preferred layout.

Design should respond to the story.

The story should not be forced into a generic section pattern.

---

## Metadata

Use only content-supported information in:

- page titles;
- descriptions;
- project summaries;
- social metadata.

Do not invent promotional claims for search or social sharing.

Keep metadata concise and accurate.

---

## Links

Use only confirmed links.

Do not invent:

- client websites;
- publication links;
- social profiles;
- project URLs.

If a required link is missing, report it.

Do not use a guessed URL.

External links should clearly behave as external links.

---

## Contact Information

Use only the approved contact details in the content files.

Do not expose information from the CV or source material merely because it is available.

Do not add:

- private addresses;
- unnecessary phone numbers;
- personal identifiers;
- unapproved social profiles.

The public portfolio should contain only intentionally selected contact information.

---

## Missing Information

If required information is missing:

1. Do not invent it.
2. Do not silently remove the affected section.
3. Use a clear development fallback only if necessary.
4. Report what is missing.

Examples include:

- project dates;
- image credits;
- links;
- publication details;
- contact details.

A missing detail is not permission to generate one.

---

## Content Review

Before considering a page complete, check:

1. Does the implementation match the relevant content file?
2. Has any copy been invented?
3. Has any meaning changed?
4. Are internal notes hidden from public view?
5. Are verification warnings preserved?
6. Has any unsupported context been presented as fact?
7. Are excerpts faithful to the source?
8. Has important content been removed for design convenience?
9. Are links and contact details confirmed?
10. Does Francis still sound like Francis?

If the answer to any of these is uncertain, stop and report the issue.

---

## Final Rule

The portfolio exists to present Francis’s writing, not to replace it.

Preserve the work.

Clarify the context.

Protect the truth.

Let the design help the writing land.
